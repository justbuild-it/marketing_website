# Section 6: "How Iriscale Works" — Image Concepts
**Date:** February 3, 2026  
**Purpose:** Visual concepts for the three-step UI mockup boxes

---

## Overall Visual Theme

The three images should tell a **progressive story** of transformation:
- **Input** → **Processing** → **Output**
- Raw information → Structured intelligence → Ready-to-use content

Each image should subtly reference the knowledge base concept — information flowing in, being organized into categories (industries, personas, products, markets), and flowing out as actionable strategies and content.

---

## Step 1: FOUNDATION
**"Expert strategy without the expert"**

### Concept: The Knowledge Funnel

**Visual Elements:**
- Multiple input sources converging into a central structure:
  - Website/URL icon
  - Document icons
  - Question/answer bubbles
- Central element: A structured repository or "brain" being built
- Output indicators showing organized categories emerging:
  - Industries
  - Buyer Personas  
  - Products & Services
  - Target Markets
  - Competitor Analysis

**Visual Metaphor Options:**

| Option | Description | Mood |
|--------|-------------|------|
| **A. Constellation Forming** | Scattered data points connecting into a structured pattern | Discovery, clarity |
| **B. Blueprint Building** | Architectural/technical drawing being assembled from inputs | Precision, expertise |
| **C. Garden Growing** | Seeds (inputs) growing into an organized garden of knowledge | Organic, nurturing |
| **D. Puzzle Completing** | Pieces clicking into place to form a complete picture | Satisfaction, wholeness |

**Key Message to Convey:**
> "You provide the raw ingredients; we build the strategic foundation."

**Color Emphasis:** Foundation/building colors — deep teal (#0b363c), grounding earth tones

---

## Step 2: STRATEGY
**"Content mapped to what customers actually need"**

### Concept: The Journey Map

**Visual Elements:**
- Persona icons/avatars at different stages
- Pathways/journey lines connecting touchpoints
- Content nodes mapped to specific journey stages
- Pain point indicators being addressed at each stage
- Strategic connections being drawn (like a network or flowchart)

**Visual Metaphor Options:**

| Option | Description | Mood |
|--------|-------------|------|
| **A. Network of Connections** | Nodes and lines showing personas connected to content strategies | Intelligence, mapping |
| **B. Chess Board** | Strategic positioning of pieces (content) for different scenarios | Strategic thinking |
| **C. River Delta** | Main strategy branching into specific channels for each persona | Flow, customization |
| **D. Control Room** | Dashboard view showing multiple persona journeys being orchestrated | Command, oversight |

**Key Message to Convey:**
> "Every piece of content has a purpose — mapped to what your customers actually need at each stage."

**Color Emphasis:** Strategy/connection colors — accent orange (#ec632f), connecting lines

---

## Step 3: EXECUTION
**"Post-ready content optimized for conversion"**

### Concept: The Content Factory Output

**Visual Elements:**
- Finished content pieces emerging/being produced:
  - Blog post preview
  - Social media post cards
  - Email template
  - Landing page snippet
- Publishing channel icons (LinkedIn, WordPress, email)
- Quality indicators (SEO score, readability, brand voice check)
- "Ready to publish" or approval checkmarks

**Visual Metaphor Options:**

| Option | Description | Mood |
|--------|-------------|------|
| **A. Assembly Line Completion** | Polished content pieces rolling off the line, ready to ship | Efficiency, scale |
| **B. Launch Pad** | Content pieces poised for launch to different platforms | Action, momentum |
| **C. Gallery Display** | Finished content pieces displayed like artwork, ready for the world | Quality, pride |
| **D. Distribution Hub** | Central point with content flowing out to multiple channels | Reach, automation |

**Key Message to Convey:**
> "From strategy to published content — just review and go."

**Color Emphasis:** Execution/completion colors — success greens, clean whites, action orange

---

## Visual Continuity Suggestions

### Connecting Elements Across All Three Images

1. **Consistent Icon Style** — Use the same visual language for representing:
   - Data/information (dots, nodes)
   - Categories (industry, persona, product icons)
   - Content outputs (document shapes)

2. **Progressive Color Shift**
   - Step 1: Cooler tones (building, foundation)
   - Step 2: Transitional (connecting, mapping)
   - Step 3: Warmer tones (action, output)

3. **Flow Direction**
   - Step 1: Inward flow (inputs converging)
   - Step 2: Lateral flow (connections spreading)
   - Step 3: Outward flow (outputs distributing)

4. **Complexity Progression**
   - Step 1: Simple → Complex (raw to structured)
   - Step 2: Complex → Organized (chaos to order)
   - Step 3: Organized → Actionable (strategy to execution)

---

## Recommended Approach

### Option Set A: Abstract/Conceptual (Minimal, Modern)
Clean geometric shapes, nodes and lines, data visualization aesthetic. Works well with the existing Figma design's modern feel.

### Option Set B: Illustrated/Metaphorical (Warmer, Approachable)  
Hand-drawn or illustrated style using real-world metaphors (garden, blueprint, factory). More personality, more memorable.

### Option Set C: Product-Forward (Realistic)
Actual simplified screenshots or wireframes of the Iriscale interface showing real UI at each stage. Most literal, builds product familiarity.
